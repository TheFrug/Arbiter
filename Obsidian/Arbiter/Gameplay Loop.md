**Compliance Interviewer** (come up with a better name than this)
Player's job is to conduct random survey/interviews of citizens. This is a small, seemingly innocuous way the state keeps tabs on citizens, by demanding they attend randomly selected interviews. It simultaneously reinforces the idea that the state is omnipresent. 
In gameplay, this means the player asks few basic questions, the answers to which are filled out in a form called the '[[Compliance Assessment Form]]'. The player asks questions, each of which move through a branching dialogue before returning to the **Base Node**, where the list of questions are asked and shrinks over time, eventually being replaced with 'I think that's everything we need from you', which begins a sequence where the subject leaves the interview, a buzzer sounds, and a new one comes in.
#### Question Nucleus
The Question nucleus, as I am ever-so-pompously calling it, is the **Base Node** of each interview question. Here, there are 4-5 questions that will be asked in each interview, each moving into its own branch. Some branches are succinct, especially with people who are not dodgy about questions, and others are longer, especially as the player gets wrapped up in conversation

#### Compliance Assessment Form
In the **Compliance Assessment Form**, in addition to the question answer slots, there is also a 4x3 grid of checkboxes the player can fill out that will influence the interview.  
'I'm Billy Blazer, you stupid pig.'
'Go ahead, check off the little box that says "I had my feelings hurt cuz I'm a lil bitch!"'

#### Efficiency
In a fuller version of the game, the player is rated on how many interviews they can conduct within the time frame of a working day. This will vary based on how efficient the player's methods are. Using Force skill checks will predispose the player to cruel, more efficient methods of information extraction.
